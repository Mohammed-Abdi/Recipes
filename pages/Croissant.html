French Butter Croissant

French Butter Croissants are flaky, buttery, and irresistibly delicious. Made with layers of laminated dough, these croissants are a staple of French baking. Perfect for breakfast or as a snack, they are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven with a cup of coffee or tea.

Instructions

1. Prepare the Dough
In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Gradually add the warm milk and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 1 hour.
2. Prepare the Butter Block
Place the cold butter between two sheets of parchment paper and pound it into a rectangle using a rolling pin. Chill the butter block in the fridge for 30 minutes.
3. Laminate the Dough
Roll out the dough into a large rectangle. Place the butter block in the center and fold the dough over it like an envelope. Roll out the dough again and fold it into thirds. Repeat this process twice, chilling the dough for 30 minutes between folds.
4. Shape the Croissants
Roll out the dough into a large rectangle and cut it into triangles. Roll each triangle from the base to the tip to form a croissant shape. Place the croissants on a baking sheet and let them rise for 1-2 hours.
5. Bake the Croissants
Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush the croissants with egg wash and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and flaky.
6. Serve
Let the croissants cool slightly before serving. Enjoy warm with butter, jam, or your favorite spread.
