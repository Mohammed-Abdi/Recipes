Sourdough Bread

PREP TIME20 Minutes
COOK TIME30 Minutes
Bread

Sourdough bread is a rustic, tangy bread made through natural fermentation, giving it a slightly sour flavor and chewy texture. The fermentation process creates a unique, flavorful loaf with a satisfying crust. It’s the perfect bread to enjoy with butter or as a sandwich base.

Instructions

1. Prepare the Sourdough Starter
If you are using a store-bought starter, make sure it's well-fed and bubbly. If making your own starter, mix flour and water and let it ferment for a few days, feeding it daily.
2. Mix Ingredients
In a large mixing bowl, combine the sourdough starter, warm water, flour, salt, sugar, and olive oil (optional). Stir to form a dough.
3. Knead the Dough
Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
4. Let the Dough Rise
Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 3 to 4 hours, or until doubled in size.
5. Shape the Dough
Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air. Shape the dough into a loaf and place it in a greased bread pan.
6. Bake the Bread
Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Bake the bread for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.
7. Cool and Serve
Allow the bread to cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes before slicing. Serve with butter, or use for sandwiches.
