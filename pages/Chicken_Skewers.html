Chicken Skewers with Slices of Sweet Peppers

COOK TIME15 Minutes

Chicken Skewers with Slices of Sweet Peppers is a delicious and colorful dish featuring juicy, marinated chicken grilled with vibrant sweet peppers. The combination of savory chicken and slightly smoky, crisp peppers creates a perfect balance of flavors. Quick to prepare, this protein-packed meal is ideal for any occasion!

Instructions

1. Prepare the Marinade
In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, smoked paprika, oregano, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until the mixture is well-blended. This marinade will coat the chicken and vegetables, adding flavor throughout the grilling process.
2. Marinate the Chicken
Add the cubed chicken to the bowl with the marinade. Use a spoon or your hands to toss the chicken pieces in the marinade until each piece is thoroughly coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes. For best results, marinate the chicken for 1-2 hours. If you're in a rush, even 30 minutes will still yield great flavor.
3. Prepare the Vegetables
While the chicken marinates, prepare the vegetables. Slice the bell peppers into 1-inch thick strips or rings, making sure each piece is large enough to stay securely on the skewer. Cut the onion into 8 wedges, keeping the root end intact to help hold the layers together. Set the vegetables aside.
4. Preheat the Grill
Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (about 375Â°F to 400Â°F or 190Â°C to 200Â°C). If you're using a grill pan, preheat it on medium-high heat on the stovetop. If you're using a charcoal grill, allow the coals to burn down to a medium heat before grilling. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 15-20 minutes prior to use to prevent them from burning during grilling.
5. Assemble the Skewers
Once the chicken has marinated, itâ€™s time to assemble the skewers. Start by threading a piece of marinated chicken onto a skewer, followed by a slice of red bell pepper, a slice of onion, a piece of yellow bell pepper, and a piece of green bell pepper. Repeat the process until the skewer is full, ensuring that the chicken and vegetables are tightly packed but not overcrowded. This helps them cook evenly. Repeat this process with the remaining skewers.
6. Grill the Skewers
Place the assembled skewers on the preheated grill. Cook the chicken skewers for about 10-12 minutes, turning them every 3-4 minutes to ensure even grilling on all sides. The chicken should have a nice golden brown color and be fully cooked through, with an internal temperature of 165Â°F (75Â°C) when measured with a meat thermometer. The vegetables should be tender with slight char marks. If you prefer extra char, you can leave the skewers on the grill for an additional 2-3 minutes. Be careful not to overcook the chicken, as it can become dry.
6. Rest and Serve
Once the chicken skewers are fully cooked, remove them from the grill and place them on a clean plate or serving dish. Let the skewers rest for about 2-3 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute and keep the chicken tender. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro for a burst of color and freshness. You can also serve the skewers with lemon wedges on the side for an extra squeeze of citrus.
7. Serve
Serve the chicken skewers with a side of warm flatbread, a fresh salad, or a serving of rice. These skewers are also great with a yogurt-based dipping sauce, like tzatziki, or a spicy harissa sauce for an extra kick.
