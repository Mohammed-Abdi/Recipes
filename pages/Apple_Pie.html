A classic American dessert featuring tender, cinnamon-spiced apples encased in a flaky, buttery double crust. This perfected recipe balances sweet and tart flavors with a golden lattice finish that's both visually stunning and deliciously crisp.

Detailed Instructions

1. Prepare the Dough
Step 1: Cube 2 ½ sticks (283g) of unsalted butter, freeze for 15 minutes. Whisk together 3 cups (360g) all-purpose flour, 1 tbsp granulated sugar, and 1 tsp salt in chilled bowl.

Step 2: Using pastry cutter, incorporate frozen butter into flour until pea-sized pieces remain. Gradually add ½ cup (120ml) ice water, mixing until dough just comes together.

Step 3: Divide dough into two disks, wrap in parchment, refrigerate for 2 hours.
2. Make Apple Filling
Step 1: Peel/core apples, slice into ¼" thick wedges. Toss with ¾ cup (150g) brown sugar, ¼ cup (30g) flour, 2 tsp cinnamon, ½ tsp nutmeg, and 1 tbsp lemon juice.

Step 2: Let macerate 45 minutes, stirring every 15 minutes to develop juices.
3. Assemble the Pie
Step 1: Roll bottom crust to 12" circle, line 9" pie dish. Trim edges leaving 1" overhang. Add filling, dot with 2 tbsp butter.

Step 2: Create lattice top: Cut rolled top crust into 1" strips using pastry wheel. Weave strips over filling, alternating directions. Crimp edges with fork.
4. Baking Process
Step 1: Brush crust with egg wash (1 egg + 1 tbsp milk). Sprinkle with turbinado sugar. Freeze pie 20 minutes.

Step 2: Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 25 minutes. Reduce to 375°F (190°C), bake 50-60 minutes until bubbling and golden.

Step 3: Cool completely on rack (4 hours minimum) before slicing.
